#####Front#####
The REDEMPTION of loſt Time.Epheſ. 5.16.Redeeme the Time, for the daies are euill.LONDON, Printed by
1. TO THE RIGHT HONORABLE SIR EDWARD COKE KNIGHT, LORD chiefe Iuſtice of the Kings Maieſties Court of Comon Plees.

1. To the Reader.

1. The Contents of the Chapters which are contained in this Treatiſe.

1. EPIGRAMMA.
THE Paſt, the Preſent, and the Time to beCould a man tell, or were there mortall wightSo farre aboue
1. The Authors (beſides ſacred Scriptures) who are cited in this preſent Treatiſe.

#####Body#####

1. CHAP. I. What a precious Iewel TIME is.

1. CHAP. 2. That we are Lords of Time, and to what end GOD did bestowe it vpon vs, and wherein wee are to imploy the ſame.

1. CHAP. 3. How GOD in his iuſt Iudgement cutteth off ſinners frō enioying the benefit of Time, who before made no reckoning thereof to profit themſelues thereby, as they ought and might haue done.

1. CHAP. 4. He that now inioyeth the benefit of TIME muſt with great feruencie and zeale labour to imploy it altogether well.

1. CHAP. 5. How worthy of reproofe idle perſons are, and who they be.

1. CHAP. 6. How the BODY detaineth in captiuitie the Time which is properly the SOVLES, and how it exalts it ſelfe and rebels againſt the SOVLE.

1. CHAP. 7. That lay perſons may lawfully enioy ſome recreations, and intertainments of mirth, ſolace and pleaſure.

1. CHAP. 8. That likewiſe it is lawfull, yea very neceſſary for religious and Eccleſiaſticall perſons to vſe ſome honeſt exerciſe, which may ſerue for intermiſsion, recreation and reſt.

1. CHAP. 9. How TIME is to bee redeemed; and who they be that detaine the ſame in captiuitie.

1. CHAP. 10. How it is to be vnderſtood that the dayes are euill, and howe that therefore TIME is to be redeemed.
